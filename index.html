MindfulChat

Neuro-Symbolic & Cognitive AI for Adaptive Stress Regulation

MindfulChat is an intelligent AI chatbot designed to help university students manage stress through personalised, real-time support. Powered by a unique blend of neural intelligence and structured reasoning, it understands not just what users feel, but the context behind their emotions — delivering more meaningful and human-like interactions. By continuously learning from user behaviour and detecting cognitive load, MindfulChat adapts to provide the right support at the right time.

Research Foundation &
Knowledge Base

Explore the literature, gaps, problem statement, objectives, methodology and technologies that underpin MindfulChat.

01 · Early Solutions

Text-Based Sentiment & ML Models

Early AI-based stress detection primarily relied on text sentiment analysis and standalone machine learning models. While effective at detecting emotional cues, these systems lacked deeper reasoning, contextual understanding, and personalisation — resulting in rigid, one-size-fits-all responses that failed to adapt to individual student needs.

02 · Multimodal Computing

Facial, Voice & Physiological Signals

Recent advances in multimodal affective computing incorporate facial expressions, voice patterns, and physiological signals such as heart rate (BPM), significantly improving stress detection accuracy. However, these systems largely remain black-box models — offering predictions without clear explanations or structured intervention strategies.

03 · Neuro-Symbolic AI

A Hybrid Intelligence Paradigm

Neuro-Symbolic AI has emerged as a transformative hybrid approach combining neural networks (for perception and pattern recognition) with symbolic reasoning (for logic, interpretability, and structured decision-making). This enables systems that not only detect stress but also reason about it, explain their decisions, and provide psychologically grounded interventions.

04 · Current Gap

Missing Unified Intelligence

Despite advances, most existing solutions fail to unify neural learning with structured symbolic reasoning — the combination essential for building trustworthy and effective mental health systems. Additionally, cognitive load detection and explainable AI-driven personalisation remain largely absent from current undergraduate support tools.

Gap 1 — Black-Box Neural Systems

Current systems rely heavily on neural-only approaches that lack transparency. In mental health contexts where clinical trust and accountability are essential, systems that cannot articulate their reasoning are severely limited and potentially unsafe for vulnerable users.

Gap 2 — Limited Multimodal Integration

Most existing tools use only text input, missing the richer picture offered by facial expressions, voice tone, and physiological signals (BPM/HRV). True understanding of student stress requires fusing all these data sources into a unified, context-aware representation.

Gap 3 — No Cognitive Load Detection

Existing systems do not account for cognitive load — the mental workload a student experiences during study. Without this dimension, stress interventions lack the contextual grounding needed to be relevant, timely, and effective.

Gap 4 — Lack of Explainable Reasoning

Current chatbots cannot explain why they suggest a particular intervention. This absence of reasoning transparency undermines user trust and prevents clinicians from validating AI-generated recommendations — critical shortcomings in a sensitive domain.

Gap 5 — Minimal Adaptive Personalisation

Most tools treat every student identically over time, failing to learn from interaction history or adapt responses based on evolving emotional patterns. Without personalisation, long-term engagement and effectiveness remain severely limited.

Undergraduate students experience dynamic stress influenced by academic, emotional, and physiological factors. Existing AI-based tools are limited in their ability to provide adaptive, explainable, and context-aware support, as they lack both multimodal understanding and structured reasoning capabilities. There is an urgent need for an intelligent system that can understand stress holistically using multiple data sources, reason about mental states using structured psychological knowledge, and deliver personalised and explainable interventions that genuinely support student wellbeing.

Primary Objective: To develop a Neuro-Symbolic and Cognitive AI chatbot that provides adaptive stress regulation for undergraduate students.

01

Detect stress using multimodal inputs — text, facial expressions, voice tone, and BPM physiological signals — for a holistic understanding of student state.

02

Estimate cognitive load in real time using passive behavioural signals to provide context-aware intervention timing and support depth.

03

Integrate neural perception models with a symbolic reasoning engine grounded in Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) frameworks for explainable responses.

04

Design and implement CBT-based intervention strategies including relaxation techniques, cognitive reframing, and task simplification guidance.

05

Enable explainable AI-driven responses with clear reasoning traces, allowing users and clinicians to understand and trust the system's recommendations.

06

Personalise interactions based on individual user history, behavioural patterns, and the effectiveness of past interventions over time.

Layer 1

Multimodal Perception (Neural AI) — Captures user state through four parallel input channels: text (transformer-based NLP), facial expressions (CNN emotion recognition), voice (pitch, tone & speech pattern analysis via Librosa), and BPM/HRV physiological signals for real-time stress inference.

Layer 2

Cognitive Load & Stress Inference — Outputs from all modalities are fused into a unified representation to estimate stress level (Low / Medium / High) and cognitive load intensity, ensuring holistic and context-aware understanding of each student's current mental state.

Layer 3

Neuro-Symbolic Reasoning (Core Intelligence) — Neural predictions are interpreted through a symbolic reasoning engine built on CBT-based structured rules. Example: High Stress + High Cognitive Load → relaxation + task simplification. Delivers explainability, consistency, and psychological grounding.

Layer 4

Memory & Personalisation — Maintains a dynamic user profile tracking stress patterns, cognitive load trends, recurring triggers, and the effectiveness of past interventions — enabling progressively more personalised and adaptive responses over time.

Layer 5

Adaptive Intervention Engine — Delivers context-aware, personalised support including CBT coping strategies, breathing and relaxation techniques, study and time management guidance, and cognitive reframing — continuously refined through user feedback.

Layer 6

Continuous Learning & Evaluation — The system improves through model evaluation (accuracy, precision, F1-score), user feedback loops, engagement metrics, and iterative refinement of both reasoning rules and personalisation strategies.

System Components

Four Integrated
AI Pillars

MindfulChat is built on four tightly coupled components that work in concert to deliver adaptive, explainable, and personalised stress regulation.

Component 01
🧠

Neuro-Symbolic Emotion Reasoning Engine

The intellectual core of MindfulChat. Fuses Transformer-based neural models for emotional pattern recognition with a Psychological Knowledge Graph for symbolic CBT-grounded reasoning. Produces transparent, human-interpretable reasoning traces — explaining why a student is experiencing a particular emotional state and selecting the most appropriate intervention.

Component 02
📊

Cognitive Load Detection via Passive Behavioral Signals

Continuously monitors implicit behavioural signals — typing rhythm, response latency, session duration, linguistic complexity, and physiological BPM/HRV data — to assess a student's current cognitive load in real time. Provides critical contextual grounding beyond explicit text, enabling more accurate and timely stress state inference without intrusive active input.

Component 03
📈

Emotion-Aware Continual Learning Engine with Personalised Trends Visualisation

Enables the system to continuously adapt to each student's evolving emotional patterns without catastrophic forgetting. Builds rich longitudinal personal profiles tracking recurring stress triggers and effective coping strategies. Delivers an intuitive wellbeing dashboard allowing students to visualise their emotional journey and celebrate progress over time.

Component 04
⚡

Adaptive Intervention Timing & Modality Selector

Determines the optimal moment and delivery method for each intervention. Weighing inferred emotional state, cognitive load, and historical engagement patterns, this component selects the most effective support type — from mindfulness and breathing exercises to time management tips, cognitive reframing, or a compassionate prompt to seek professional help when needed.
